Understanding PSI Rules

Personal Services Income (PSI) applies when a company or trust earns income that is mainly a reward for an individual's personal skills or efforts. This covers the vast majority of IT, consulting, engineering, legal, and finance contractors.

The PSI Tests (applied in order)

Step 1 — Results Test Best outcome

PSI rules do not apply if all three are true:

  1. You are engaged to produce a specific result (not paid by the hour or day)
  2. You provide your own tools and equipment
  3. You are liable to fix defects at your own cost

⚠ Most time-rate contractors fail this test because they are paid for time, not a result.

Step 2 — 80% Rule

If 80%+ of income comes from one client, you must pass one of:

  • Unrelated clients test: Services to 2+ unrelated clients via genuine public advertising
  • Employment test: You employ someone doing 20%+ of the principal work
  • Business premises test: You use premises separate from home and client

⚠ Single-person contractors working for one client at a time almost always fail all three.

What Happens When PSI Applies to Your Pty Ltd

  • ✘ Company cannot retain profit at 25% company tax rate — income is attributed to you personally
  • ✘ Cannot split income with family members
  • ✔ Director salary and super deductions still apply
  • ✔ Legitimate business expenses still deductible
  • ✔ FBT-exempt EV benefit is unaffected by PSI
